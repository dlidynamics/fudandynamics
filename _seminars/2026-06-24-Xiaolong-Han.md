abstract: |
  Let M be a closed hyperbolic manifold. We first explain how the rigidity of unipotent flow affects the distribution and topological properties of totally geodesic hypersurfaces in M. In particular, all but finitely many properly immersed totally geodesic hypersurfaces must cut M into balls. We then explain why these properties also hold for non homogeneous submanifolds, if they are sufficiently close to homogenous ones. We then give some applications, including the construction of non totally geodesic but asymptotically geodesic hypersurfaces in higher dimensional hyperbolic manifolds.   
  This partially answers a question of Fernando Al Assal and Ben Lowe regarding the gap of hypersurfaces in higher dimensional hyperbolic manifolds and is based on a joint work with Ruojing Jiang.
